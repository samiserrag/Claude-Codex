Design notes (mockup-only — what changed from v1)

Same semantic discipline; recalibrated visual treatment per GPT/Sami feedback.

  • Palette shift: three deliberate accents (sage state, amber role, copper boundary) from a related warm family. Replaced v1's flat teal with intentional color choices that signal craft.
  • Typography hierarchy: larger headline (28px), more weight variation (400/500/600/700), generous tracking on labels. Reads as "someone designed this" instead of "default browser stylesheet."
  • Step timeline: 5-dot horizontal track with named states. The current step (Approver) is highlighted in amber with a subtle ring. Communicates progress without a progress ring that could imply approval.
  • Hero card: subtle radial gradient (one designed moment, not gradients-everywhere). 12px radius vs 6-8px on subordinate cards establishes hierarchy.
  • Boundary block: styled as a deliberate component with custom shield SVG, 3px left border, ✕ markers on each item. Not a warning sticker.
  • Custom inline SVGs: brand star, key icon (role), shield (boundary). All inline, no external assets. Replaces v1's emoji.
  • Breathing room: 24-32px section spacing, 24px card padding, generous line-height. v1 was too dense.
  • Action treatment: primary button is sage green (calm, considered — not "GO" green). Secondary button has substance, not just a text link.

Still single-panel, no vanity metrics, no three-panel layout, no progress rings for approval states, no drag-drop, no auto-actions. Semantic discipline from v1 carries forward verbatim.
